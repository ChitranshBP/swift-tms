Breakthrough Therapy

Redefining Depression Treatment

SPRAVATO® (esketamine) is the first FDA-approved nasal spray medication, taken with an oral antidepressant, for adults with treatment-resistant depression (TRD) and depressive symptoms in adults with major depressive disorder (MDD) with suicidal thoughts or actions.

Unlike traditional antidepressants that can take weeks to show results, Spravato works differently by targeting the NMDA receptor, helping to restore neural connections in the brain faster and more effectively.

The Journey

What to Expect During Your Treatment

1

Clinical Consultation

We perform a thorough evaluation to ensure Spravato is the right choice for your specific needs and medical history.

2

Treatment Session

You'll self-administer the nasal spray under the supervision of our medical staff in a private, comfortable suite.

3

Relaxation & Monitoring

You'll remain in our clinic for 2 hours to rest as the treatment takes effect, while we monitor your vital signs.

4

Same-Day Recovery

After the observation period, you'll need a pre-arranged ride home. Most patients return to normal activities the following day.

Important Safety Info

Spravato can cause temporary dissociation (feeling detached from yourself or surroundings).

A temporary increase in blood pressure may occur shortly after administration.

Common side effects include dizziness, nausea, lethargy, and a metallic taste.

A designated driver is required for transportation post-treatment.

Spravato Therapy FAQs

Spravato (esketamine) is an FDA-approved nasal spray, unlike IV ketamine which is used off-label. Spravato has undergone rigorous clinical trials and is administered with a standardized dosing protocol. It is also more commonly covered by insurance.
Many insurance plans, including Medicare and some commercial insurers, cover Spravato. Our team will verify benefits and obtain prior authorization. We also offer patient savings programs and payment plans.
Each session requires at least 2 hours. The nasal spray administration takes 5-10 minutes, followed by a 2-hour observation period in a comfortable, private suite.
Many patients report improvement within hours to days. Treatment is typically twice weekly for the first month, then weekly or bi-weekly. Clinical studies showed significant improvement in depression scores.
