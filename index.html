Rapid Relief Protocol

What is the Accelerated TMS Protocol?

Accelerated TMS delivers theta burst stimulation for 9 minutes at a time, multiple times a day, for 6 days spread over 2 weeks. Traditional Deep TMS delivers theta burst stimulation for 3 minutes once a day for 6 weeks. The accelerated protocol reduces the traditional 20-visit acute treatment phase to just 6 half days, followed by weekly maintenance visits for 4 weeks.

Because these sessions do not need to be continuous, this approach is easy to work into your schedule, effectively reducing clinic visits in the acute phase by approximately 70%.

Accelerated TMS FAQs

Each theta burst stimulation session lasts approximately 9 minutes. Multiple sessions are delivered throughout the day over 6 consecutive treatment days, with rest periods between sessions.
While standard Deep TMS requires daily sessions over 6 weeks, Accelerated TMS compresses the acute phase into just 6 days of intensive treatment across 2 weeks. Clinical studies show comparable or superior response and remission rates with the accelerated approach.
Side effects are similar to standard TMS, like mild scalp discomfort or headache during initial sessions that typically resolve quickly. No sedation is required, and you can drive yourself to and from appointments.
